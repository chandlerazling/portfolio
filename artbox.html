Overview

Artists are constantly moving from one social media platform to the next and face many issues with uploading and sharing their works. I wanted to create an experience that is specifically catered to artists and fulfills their needs in sharing work in both a casual and a professional context.

User Personas

I interviewed three artists and used them to create user personas. One was an artist who uses different social media to share her work, one was experienced in art but has only uploaded work for a portfolio site, and the last was a new artist who was looking to find artists to follow and get inspiration from.

User Flows

My focus was on uploading and collaborating on work. I realized collaboration, especially on a work level, was not needed for an MVP. I also took out notes, which I think would be a nice feature to have later on as to share ideas or create tutorials. The MVP I ended up with was a simple platform to upload work, follow users, and use uploaded work to create a portfolio site.


Wireframes

What are the user needs? Upload work and interact with other users. This is what I had concentrated into my dashboard navigation.

Artbox is not just a social media platform - it can be used in a professional context. While each user has a public profile, they have the option to upgrade their account and create a separate site to showcase their work. The first tier is Portfolio, which allows a user to create a template portfolio site routed to their own domain (or a default Artbox supplied domain). The next tier allows users to design the site themselves with a drag and drop style designer.



Mockups & Prototypes

I wanted to go for clean and modern look and otherwise my mockups stayed consistent with my wireframes. After doing some user testing, I changed some of the icons and the process for updating account settings.


User Testing

I had users interact with the homepage, dashboard, and account settings page. All of the feedback was positive for navigating the dashboard and account settings. However, there was a lot of confusing with the homepage and as to what type of site Artbox is. Every user was unsure as to whether or not they could use the site for free and why exactly they would need to pay a subscription. I learned that I needed to make it more clear that subscriptions are optional and only for creating a portfolio while uploading artwork and following other artists is completely free.

Conclusion

I think the overall flow and navigation of Artbox works well and also fulfills the top needs of artists who want to post work online. I was the most satisfied with my dashboard page as I feel that it is simple and easy to navigate. The homepage could still improve and better advertise what Artbox is about.
