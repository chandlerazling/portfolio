Overview

Vivatrix is a project I took on to practice branding, UI design, and front end development. It is a simple landing page that showcases the product.

Wireframes

After doing some research and analysis of other landing pages that advertise supplements, I wireframed two versions of the site. The main elements I wanted to include were an overview of the product, the results it has, an explanation of how it works, and testimonials. After getting feedback, I decided that the second version would give a better introduction to the product.

Branding

For the logo, I sketched out designs that and then created them in sketch. I settled on a design that was in the middle of my variations as it had a nice balance between sharp edges and curves.

I went through different variations of the brand name and decided on Vivatrix. I chose green for its associations with cleanliness, nature, and energy.

Styling

I decided on an analogous color scheme as I felt that the cool colors created a sense of healing and revitalization. For the font, I chose Nanum Gothic since it is a clean and modern font.

Mockup

For the most part, implementing the style and creating the mockup was a smooth process. I separated sections by background color, switching between white and combinations of the color scheme. The section that went through the most changes was the testimonials section. Originally, I had white text on the green background, which was difficult to read unless it was bold. I tried out different versions of white backgrounds for the individual testimonials and in my final design I separated the testimonial from the user into a white speech bubble.

Development

I developed a responsive page using HTML, CSS, and JQuery. I also experimented with motion design using the Velocity framework. I tried out different animations for elements as they come into view when scrolling. In addition, I used canvases for the "How It Works" section to animate the lines going between the different stages.

Conclusion

This project was a good exercise for both design and development and I learned a lot. I think that overall my design works, but I could go through more iterations of the motion design. If I had more time, I would definitely work on the animations more as there are so many subtleties to motion design. There is still a lot more to learn about timing and easing, and how changing those affects the user experience.
